--- layout: post title: 'Adding @anywhere to Your blog, or How I Pretend Like I'm a Twitter Developer' sourceurl: http://audreywatters.com/2010/04/14/adding-anywhere-to-your-blog-or-how-i-became-a-twitter-developer/ image: http://hackedu.s3.amazonaws.com/wp-contentuploads201004twitter1a.png --- Today was Twitter's first developers' conference, Chirp. They made a lot of interesting announcements, including the release of some pretty impressive statistics. They've added a developer channel which contains a lot of information for those planning to develop Twitter-based applications.

Following their incredibly easy directions, I've added the new @anywhere feature to this blog.

Step 1: Head to http://dev.twitter.com

Step 2: Don't read the documentation.

You'll be offered the choice to "read the documentation." I'm pretty sure if you want to be a developer -- no matter the platform -- you skip that bit.

Step 3: Fill out the application

Step 4: The application will generate some javascript.

Step 5: Copy the javascript (from the <script src...> portion to the </script> bit) into the header of your blog (that is, between the <head> and the </head>.

Step 6: Press "save" and hope to hell you haven't screwed up your website irreparably.

Step 8: You're set. You can now hover over Twitter names on your webpage to generate more information about them, to follow them, etc. There are other features of @anywhere too, but ya know, I didn't read the documentation.
