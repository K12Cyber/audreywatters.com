I've got tons to say.

Thoughts on Formspring. Thoughts on Facebook. Thoughts on open source. Thoughts on living with another geek. Thoughts on working from home. Thoughts on the new New Pornographers album. Thoughts on Apple closing down Lala. Thoughts on the novel I'm reading (The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo -- on my iPad). Thoughts on Spamalot and genetics and Dr. Who and 99 cent Flake Bars and what our grandfathers would say about us now.

I've got index cards strewn across my desk, scribbled with thoughts on potential blog topics, things I should research, things I should read. I've got wikis and Google Docs and Evernote notes.

I've far from exhausted my pool of ideas, resources, words.

Someone commented the other day on one of my RWW posts, challenging the authority with which I wrote on a particular topic. Fair enough. But now I think about that in terms of my professional and my personal and my public posts.

And I think about the space I want (I need) to carve out for me, for my writing, for my new writing life.
